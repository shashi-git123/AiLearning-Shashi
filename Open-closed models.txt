How many open source models are there?
There are over 2.9 million AI models hosted on the internet today, according to live data from Hugging Face Models. The overwhelming majority of these are open-weight or open-source community variants

How many closed source models are there?
There is no exact, static number of closed-source AI models because new proprietary models are continuously being developed, updated, and deployed by tech companies globally. 
OpenAI: Creators of the GPT series 
Google DeepMind: Developers behind the Gemini ecosystem (including Ultra, Pro, and Flash variants).
Anthropic: The team responsible for the Claude family (such as Claude 3.5 Sonnet and Opus series).
Microsoft: Deploys proprietary models like the Copilot foundational features and specialized enterprise variants.

Open Source: The model's weights (the learned data parameters) and code are publicly shared. Anyone can download, run, and modify the model locally.
Closed Source: The model is a trade secret. It is hosted entirely on the provider's servers. Users can only interact with it through an API or a web interface.
Open Source: Maximum privacy. Data never leaves your personal machine or private corporate servers. This makes it ideal for highly regulated industries like healthcare or finance.
Closed Source: Lower control. Your prompts and data must be sent over the internet to a third-party server (like OpenAI or Google). You must trust their data privacy policies.

Open Source: Requires technical expertise to deploy, configure, and maintain. Historically lagged behind closed models, though the gap has narrowed significantly.
Closed Source: Instant, plug-and-play setup via simple APIs. Typically offers the highest frontier capabilities for reasoning, speed, and complex multimodal tasks.
